Orbi and the Last Color of the Universe — full story

A science-fiction picture book about kindness and sharing, for children aged 4 to 9. Part of the proceeds support charity.

Long, long ago, in a quiet corner of space, the universe began to lose its colors. One morning the red went out, then the blue fell asleep, and the yellow flew away like a frightened bird.

On a small grey moon lived Orbi, a girl with hair full of curls and pockets full of bolts. Her best friend was Spark, a little tin firefly that glowed ever so faintly.

"If nobody does anything, the whole universe will be grey," said Orbi. "Let's go find the lost colors!" With an old can, three springs and lots of imagination, she built a rocket the size of a dog.

First stop: the Planet of the Silent Mountains. There lived a sad and lonely giant who hadn't spoken to anyone for a hundred years. Orbi gave him her scarf and sat down to listen. The giant smiled for the first time in a century… and the mountains turned orange!

"The color wasn't lost!" thought Orbi. "It was sleeping, waiting for one small act of kindness."

Second stop: the Planet of the Fishless Sea, dry and cracked. Orbi and Spark dug until they found a hidden spring and shared it with everyone. The sea filled up again… and shone a beautiful blue!

Third stop: the Asteroid of the Forgotten Children. Orbi shared her snack and her bolts, and let Spark glow for everyone. Laughter filled the asteroid… and a warm yellow was born, like a tiny little sun.

Planet after planet, Orbi discovered a secret: the colors of the universe aren't found… they're given. Every time someone shares, helps or comforts, a new color is born.

And so, little by little, the universe shone again: red from hugs, green from sharing, violet from forgiving, and gold from love. Today, when you look up at the stars, remember: you too keep a hidden color. And the universe waits, patiently, for you to give it away.
